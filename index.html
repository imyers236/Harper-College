Phishing Attacks

Phishing uses email, phone calls, texts or malicious websites to solicit personal information by posing as a trustworthy organization. For example, an attacker may send an email from a seemingly reputable credit card company that requests your account information. Usually under the guise that there is a problem or a need of information.

Phishing Emails might have

  • spelling errors
  • grammatical errors
  • uncommon phrasing
  • Say it is urgent

How to Avoid Phishing

  • Avoid emailing your personal information
  • Hover over links to see where they go
  • Check the sender's address matches the email's contents Ex: a credit card company will not use @hotmail to email you
  • If unsure contact the company directly

Examples of Phishing

  • Emails will say your password has been compromised and gives a link to change the password.

Protect Yourself

▼

Password Guideline:

  • Harper account passwords must be between 6-20 characters and contain both letters and numbers
  • Use both upper and lowercase letter, number, and special characters
  • Avoid putting PII (Ex: name or birthdate) into your password to make it harder to guess
  • Research indicates a longer, more memorable password can provide better security than a shorter password with added complexity
  • Passwords should not be reused for different accounts or websites. If one of your accounts gets compromised, then all the other accounts that use the same login name and password or even just the password can easily be compromised.
  • To avoid forgetting passwords or reusing them, get a password manager that can hold them for you

Securing Mobile Devices

  • Configure a passcode to gain access to and use the device.
  • Leave your Bluetooth turned off when you are not using it. Bluetooth connections should be done in private, uncrowded areas.
  • Enroll your device in Find My iPhone or an equivalent service. This will help you locate your device should it be lost or stolen.
  • Install security updates.
  • Backup your data regularly. It is recommended to sync your mobile device with your computer.

Identity Theft

Identity theft is a serious crime. Identity theft happens when someone uses information about you without your permission. They could use your:

  • Name and address
  • Credit card or bank account numbers
  • Social security number
  • Medical insurance account numbers

Some warning signs that you may be a victim of identity theft are:

  • Errors on your credit or bank statements
  • Errors on your credit report
  • Late or missing bills
  • Receiving credit cards you did not apply for
  • Calls or letters about purchases you did not make
  • Denials of credit or being offered less favorable terms for no apparent reason

If you believe your identity has been stolen, a helpful guide is available from the Federal Trade Commission at www.identitytheft.gov.

Working with Student and Employee PII

▼

Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

Personally identifiable information (PII) is information that can be used to locate or identify an individual. Loss of such information may lead to identity theft or other fraudulent use.

Examples of Work PII include:

  • Student's/Employee's Full Name
  • Student's/Employee's Address
  • Personal Email Address
  • Social Security Number
  • H number
  • Student's/Colleagues Phone Number
  • Work Credit Card Number

Before including PII think:

  • Is this information necessary for the topic?
  • Is this the right place to include PII?
  • Does this person/people need this PII?

How should PII be handled:

  • PII should only be stored in private protected drives assigned by the college
  • Do not store PII in personal cloud accounts such as Google drive, Drop box, etc. or non-Harper issued devices; such as laptops or phones
  • Avoid putting PII in emails (even to send to one's self) or other forms of unprotected communication such as text

Ferpa Requirements:

No one shall have access to, nor will the institution disclose, any information from students' education records - other than Directory Information - without the written consent of students, except to officials of other institutions in which students seek to enroll; to persons or organizations providing students financial aid; or providing enrollment and/or degree verification to accrediting agencies carrying out the accreditation function; in compliance with a judicial order; in emergency situations when necessary to protect the health or safety of students or other persons; and to those members of the College community with a legitimate educational interest.

"Legitimate educational interest" means and includes a demonstrable need to know by any staff member in terms of his or her assigned duties.

All Harper College employees are required to protect and secure documents containing sensitive information.

Why is my email Quarantined

▼

Harper uses Microsoft to flag emails that contain PII when sent to outside email addresses. These emails are then reviewed by IT security staff.

What information can cause my email to be quarantined?

An individual's first name or first initial and last name in combination with any one or more of the following data elements

  • Driver's license number or State identification card number
  • Student's/Employee's home Address
  • Personal Email Address
  • Social Security Number
  • H number
  • Medical information
  • Health insurance information
  • Unique biometric data
  • Student's/Employee's Personal Phone Number
  • Bank Account number, credit or debit card number
    • Or
  • Username or email address, in combination with a password or security question

How to avoid my email being quarantined

  • Does this information need to be sent through email?
  • Does this person/people need this PII?
  • Remove the PII
  • Encrypt the email before sending - Send Encrypted Emails
    • If your email is found to have PII, you will receive an email saying your email was not sent due to the PII and how to encrypt or remove the PII.
